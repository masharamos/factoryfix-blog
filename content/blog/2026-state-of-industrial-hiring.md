Every year, FactoryFix publishes a comprehensive look at the state of industrial hiring in the United States. This year's report draws on more data than ever before.

## What we looked at

We analyzed over 2 million job postings across manufacturing, construction, and logistics — and paired that with survey data from 1,200 hiring managers to understand what's actually happening on the ground.

## The headline finding

67% of hiring managers said finding qualified skilled trades candidates was harder in 2025 than the year before. That number has increased every year since we started tracking it in 2021.

## What's driving it

The pipeline problem isn't new. Retirements are outpacing new entrants into the trades by a wide margin, and the perception gap — the idea that trades careers are less desirable than four-year degree paths — continues to suppress interest among younger workers.

## What the best teams are doing differently

The hiring teams outperforming their peers share a few things in common: they move fast, they use technology to expand their sourcing reach, and they treat candidate experience as seriously as they treat customer experience.
