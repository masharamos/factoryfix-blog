For years, staffing firms struggled to crack the skilled trades market. The problem wasn't effort — it was approach.

## The old playbook wasn't working

Traditional staffing relies on a large pool of active job seekers. Skilled trades workers, especially experienced ones, are rarely active. They're employed, often happy, and not browsing job boards. You have to find them where they are.

## What changed

The firms winning today are using AI-powered sourcing tools to identify passive candidates — people who aren't looking but who match the profile exactly. Combined with mobile-first outreach and faster response times, they're connecting with candidates their competitors never reach.

## The speed factor

In skilled trades, the best candidates are off the market in days. Staffing firms that have cut their time-to-submit from a week to 24 hours are seeing dramatically better fill rates.

> The recruiters who wait for candidates to come to them are losing. The ones going to where candidates actually are — that's where the edge is.

## What this means for your firm

If your current process relies primarily on job board applicants, you're competing for the same small pool as everyone else. The firms pulling ahead are the ones who've built a proactive, technology-assisted sourcing motion.
